Workflow Instructions

Please read these steps carefully before beginning the translation review.

1

Phase 1: Baseline Analysis

  • Source Text Review: Read the original Arabic medical abstract. (Note: You can click on highlighted medical terms to view their definitions).
  • MT Evaluation: Read the raw English machine translation (MT) to identify glaring errors. Note if any long, complex Arabic sentences caused the MT to break down structurally.
2

Phase 2: The "Physician's Pass"

Student-Led Editing as Subject Matter Expert

  • Terminology: Manually correct medical terms, acronyms, and specialized vocabulary that the MT mistranslated.
  • Semantic Accuracy: Ensure the exact meaning of the Arabic abstract is preserved. Correct instances where the MT changed medical findings/data.
  • Action: Paste your corrected version into the Phase 2 input box in the workspace.
3

Phase 3: The "Linguistic Broker"

AI-Assisted Editing

  • Prompt Engineering: Provide your Phase 2 text to an AI with this strict prompt:
    "Act as a professional academic proofreader. Review this medical abstract for surface-level issues only: syntax, grammar, spelling, and academic style. Do not alter the specific medical terminology or change the core semantic meaning of the data."
  • Action: Paste the AI's final output into the Phase 3 input box in the workspace.
4

Phase 4: Automatic Annotation

  • Comparison: Click the "Run Analysis" button. The system will compare Phase 2 and Phase 3.
  • Tagging (Laflamme framework): It will automatically attempt to classify edits across dimensions:
    • Operation: Addition, Deletion, or Replacement.
    • Linguistic Dimension: Syntax, Style, or Punctuation.
    • Linguistic Unit: Word, Phrase, etc.
5

Phase 5: Final Synthesis

  • Data Analysis: Review the automated tags. Did the AI over-edit for style? Did it hallucinate?
  • Conclusion: Reflect on whether AI effectively replaces the human proofreader and identify its blind spots.
